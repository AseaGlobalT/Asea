Why IL-1β matters

IL-1β is a pro-inflammatory cytokine tied to chronic low-grade inflammation — what researchers call "inflammaging." It's linked to joint discomfort, fatigue, immune dysfunction, and accelerated aging. An 89% average reduction after 16 weeks is a striking finding, especially in people who were already healthy at baseline.

Why Glutathione matters

Glutathione is your body's primary internal antioxidant and detoxification molecule. It can't be effectively supplemented directly — the body has to make it. A 14.4% increase suggests ASEA is activating the cellular machinery to produce more, not just adding antioxidants from outside.

The Nrf2 connection

Independent research from the University of Bath (UK) and Western Sydney University (Australia) found that redox signaling molecules significantly activated the Nrf2 pathway — the master regulator of antioxidant gene expression and stress resilience. This is the mechanism behind the glutathione increase.

Study design details

IRB-approved, GCP-compliant. 30 participants, equal male/female. 4 oz twice daily on an empty stomach. Comprehensive panels: lipids, glucose, liver, kidney, hematology, inflammatory cytokines. All clinical chemistry values stayed within normal reference ranges throughout.
